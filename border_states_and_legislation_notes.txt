KEY CONCEPTUAL REFRAME (read first)
===================================
"Going wet" in Arkansas is NOT a single binary. It is a BUNDLE of
margins that switch on at different dates and geographies:

  1. OFF-PREMISE RETAIL  -- liquor stores (capped 1 per 7,500 pop),
     unlimited beer/wine permits, beer/wine in grocery/convenience.
     Turns on at the COUNTYWIDE wet vote. <-- this is your treatment.
  2. ON-PREMISE BEER/WINE in restaurants (no private club needed).
     Also turns on at the countywide vote.
  3. ON-PREMISE LIQUOR-BY-THE-DRINK (bars, mixed drinks, spirits).
     SEPARATE sub-county margin. Pre-2013 cohorts needed a separate
     city/town local-option election. After Act 1008 (2013), a city
     in a county that went wet AFTER 1 Nov 2012 with >=100 active ABC
     permits could authorize LBD by ordinance instead. Staggered,
     sub-county, and later than the countywide vote.
  4. PRIVATE CLUBS -- existed in "dry" counties since ~2003 (ABC began
     issuing private-club permits). On-premise consumption was NOT
     zero at baseline; Benton Co. had the most private clubs in the
     state before its 2012 vote. Craighead/Faulkner are dry today but
     full of "bar and grill" private-club permits.
  5. SUNDAY SALES -- legalized alongside the transitions. Relevant to
     the monthly/DOW panel.

Implications:
  - Your off-premise treatment date is the CLEAN margin. Good choice;
    the institutional record supports it.
  - The on/off-premise heterogeneity you want is genuinely messy:
    LBD is sub-county and time-varying, and the OFFICIAL Arkansas GIS
    "ABC Wet and Dry Areas" shapefile explicitly does NOT encode on-
    vs off-premise. To split on/off you need ABC PERMIT-TYPE records,
    not the shapefile.
  - "Dry" never meant "no access." Many border counties had out-of-
    state off-premise access (below); all dry counties had private-
    club on-premise. The travel-cost reduction from going wet is
    therefore heterogeneous across treated counties by construction.


SECTION A -- BORDER-STATE OFF-PREMISE ACCESS CODING RULES
=========================================================
What matters for distance/contamination is OFF-PREMISE availability
across the line, not on-premise. Code the out-of-state ring (pull
exact out-of-state neighbors from your Census county-adjacency file,
which already includes cross-state adjacencies) using these rules:

MISSOURI (north border)
  No dry counties. Wet statewide, entire 2008-2023 window, incl.
  Sunday. -> Every AR northern-tier border county had off-premise
  access to the north THROUGHOUT. Confidence: HIGH.
  AR counties touching MO: Benton (treated), Carroll, Boone (treated),
  Marion, Baxter, Fulton, Randolph (treated), Clay.

OKLAHOMA (west border)
  Package/liquor stores existed in ALL OK counties the entire window,
  including pre-2018. SQ 792 (effective 1 Oct 2018) added full-strength
  beer/wine in grocery/convenience stores and enabled restaurant/bar
  liquor-by-the-drink in formerly "dry" (= no-LBD) counties. That is an
  ON-PREMISE / retail-format change, NOT an off-premise availability
  change. -> For OFF-PREMISE, OK = wet throughout. The 2018 date only
  matters if you ever model on-premise/bar access across the border.
  Confidence: HIGH. (This confirms your prior "OK pre-2018 non-affecting"
  note and gives the reason.)
  AR counties touching OK: Benton (treated), Little River (treated),
  Sevier (treated), Polk (treated), Washington, Crawford, Sebastian,
  Scott, Montgomery, Howard.

TEXAS (southwest; Bowie Co./Texarkana)
  Bowie County wet for off-premise. TX uses precinct-level local option
  elsewhere, but the AR-facing area (Texarkana) is wet. Confidence:
  MED-HIGH; verify exact TX neighbor counties via adjacency file.
  AR counties touching TX: Miller, Little River (treated).

LOUISIANA (south border)
  Mostly wet: 51 wet parishes, 15 wet-with-some-dry-wards; 27 parishes
  ban Sunday sales. Union Parish (faces Columbia) -> verify, likely wet.
  Confidence: MED; verify the specific facing parishes.
  AR counties touching LA: Miller, Lafayette, Columbia (treated), Union,
  Ashley, Chicot.

MISSISSIPPI (southeast; across the Mississippi River)
  Patchwork. Possession legal statewide since 2020 (HB 1087) but SALES
  still require local option; ~26 dry-for-liquor counties as of 2025.
  CROSSING CONSTRAINT DOMINATES (see below) -- only bridge crossings
  matter, so MS county status is largely moot except near a crossing.

TENNESSEE (northeast; across the Mississippi River)
  Liquor dry-by-default for many counties, but BEER is widely available
  regardless of wet/dry, and Shelby Co. (Memphis) is wet. CROSSING
  CONSTRAINT DOMINATES -- West Memphis (Crittenden) <-> Memphis is the
  major crossing.

RIVER-CROSSING CONSTRAINT (critical for E/SE and parts of S/SW)
  Straight-line Haversine distance (your R=3958.8 method) OVERSTATES
  accessibility wherever the border is the Mississippi or Red River and
  no bridge exists. For E/SE AR counties, route distance-to-nearest-wet
  through actual crossings, or restrict cross-river wet neighbors to
  counties reachable by a bridge. Major Mississippi-River crossings:
  West Memphis (I-40/I-55, Crittenden Co.); Helena area; Lake Village /
  US-82 (Chicot/Desha) to MS. Without this correction, E/SE counties
  will look closer to out-of-state wet access than they are.

PRIMARY-SOURCE VERIFICATION (for the "exact legislation" gap)
  - Arkansas ABC Division: permit-type records by county/date give the
    on/off-premise and LBD timing the GIS shapefile omits.
  - Arkansas GIS "ABC Wet and Dry Areas" (gis.arkansas.gov, updated after
    Nov general elections): authoritative geography incl. townships/wards/
    cities -> use for sub-county/moist coding, NOT on/off-premise.
  - County clerk records: official local-option status per A.C.A.
  - The 38% registered-voter petition threshold is the selection
    mechanism -- worth a sentence in the paper (treated counties are the
    ones that could organize past a high bar; not random).


SECTION C -- WHERE BORDER DATA ENTERS THE PIPELINE (ranked)
===========================================================
1. DISTANCE-TO-NEAREST-WET test (your pending median-split-at-28.2-mi
   task). Distances are currently to nearest IN-STATE wet county only.
   For border counties (esp. W and N) the true nearest off-premise
   source is out-of-state and CLOSER. Recompute centroid distances
   against AR-wet + out-of-state-wet (crossing-adjusted) BEFORE running.
   This likely reshuffles the 7-close/5-far split. HIGHEST PRIORITY.

2. TREATMENT CONTAMINATION / "deep-dry" robustness. Border dry counties
   with out-of-state off-premise access had a SMALLER travel-cost drop
   when going wet than interior dry counties. Add a split: treated
   counties WITH vs WITHOUT a pre-treatment out-of-state wet neighbor
   (Benton, Little River, Sevier, Polk, Columbia all border out-of-state
   wet). Directly answers the workshop "they'd already be treated" point.

3. SPILLOVER/NEIGHBOR construction. A dry AR neighbor that ALSO borders
   an out-of-state wet county had its nearest access UNCHANGED by the AR
   transition -> should not be coded as spillover-exposed (or flag it).
   Augment n_wet_neighbors / share_wet_neighbors / any_wet_neighbor to
   include out-of-state wet neighbors.

4. MAPS/FIGURES. Shade a one-county ring of out-of-state counties by
   wet/dry; mark the river crossings. Visually resolves the "no isolated
   dry county, no never-treated" framing the audiences kept probing.


SECTION D -- WORKSHOP ACTION ITEMS (both transcripts)
=====================================================
HIGH VALUE
  - FARS vehicle registration -> resident vs visitor test for the rural
    ATTRACTOR hypothesis. FARS reliably has vehicle REGISTRATION STATE
    (tests out-of-state inflow). County-of-origin is NOT a standard FARS
    field -- verify what your vintage carries before promising the
    within-AR cross-county version. (Raised twice: Max / Speaker 12.)
  - Disjoint outcome panels (Speaker 6): report NON-alcohol fatal
    (= fatal minus alcohol) and alcohol fatal SEPARATELY, not all-fatal
    (which contains alcohol). Cleaner mechanism mapping; the distance
    channel should move non-alcohol fatals too via total VMT.
  - Non-fatal crashes + DUIs as outcomes (Speaker 4 / Nick): distinguishes
    "drinking less" vs "drinking closer," and adds power (fatal crashes
    are rare, ~6.6/county-yr). Source: ARDOT for non-fatal/total crashes;
    DUI arrests are public.
  - Identification framing (Speaker 2, emphatic): DON'T frame as classic
    border-discontinuity (assumes non-travel). This is CROSS-BORDER
    SHOPPING / tax-avoidance. Cite the gas-tax border literature (people
    far from the border are untreated; a treatment window in distance).
    Re-label your "border-discontinuity robustness" accordingly. Ties to
    the distance-to-nearest-wet design (#1 above) and Speaker 9's
    distance-to-border-as-intensity suggestion.

MEDIUM VALUE
  - Year-8 spike (Speaker 4): almost certainly a COMPOSITION artifact --
    only the earliest cohorts (Boone/Clark 2010) reach t+8; far-horizon
    estimates ride on 1-2 counties (see your fig5 cohort-composition).
    Cap the event-study window where >=3-4 cohorts are present; don't
    over-interpret the spike.
  - Rural/urban cutoff (Speaker 12): 20k is arbitrary. Use USDA Rural-
    Urban Continuum Codes (RUCC) or Census urban-share instead, and
    report the split's sensitivity.
  - Church density (Speaker 11): control for DENOMINATION/sect, not just
    churches_pc. Southern Baptist vs Catholic differ sharply on
    prohibition support. ARDA / Religion Census denominational shares
    are a refinement over CBP NAICS 813110.
  - Selection (Speaker 6): switching wet isn't random (38% petition
    threshold). DiD doesn't fix selection on time-varying unobservables;
    lean on NYT + pre-trends, and consider a matched / synthetic-control
    comparison ("a county like this that stayed dry") as robustness.
  - Sunday-sales literature (Andy/Speaker 1): add to lit review -- all
    treated counties legalized Sunday sales; supports the monthly/DOW
    panel. (He also offered to dig up a spillover paper -- follow up.)

THEORY
  - Disutility term: the audience (and Udall) converged on adding the
    disutility of accident/penalty -- you've added the lambda*pi*kappa
    term. Keep it; just lock the comparative-static signs (you flagged a
    dA/dP (-) vs dP/dD_c (+) inconsistency to fix). The multiplicative
    impaired-miles form m = alpha*a + gamma*a*d, with gamma*a*d -> 0 on
    going wet, is the clean version.
